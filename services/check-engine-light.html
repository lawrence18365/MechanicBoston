Common Causes of Check Engine Lights

⚠️

Loose Gas Cap

A simple loose or damaged gas cap can trigger the check engine light due to fuel system pressure issues.

⚠️

Oxygen Sensor Failure

Failed O2 sensors can cause poor fuel economy and increased emissions.

⚠️

Catalytic Converter Issues

A failing catalytic converter can cause poor performance and failed emissions tests.

⚠️

Mass Airflow Sensor Problems

MAF sensor issues can cause stalling, poor acceleration, and reduced fuel efficiency.

⚠️

Ignition System Failure

Problems with spark plugs, coils, or wires can trigger the check engine light.

⚠️

EVAP System Leaks

Evaporative emission control system leaks commonly trigger check engine lights.
